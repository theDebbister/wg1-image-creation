Farsi/Arabic integration: solutions summary

1. Letter shaping and connections

Problem: Drawing characters individually breaks letter connections (Arabic letters only join when rendered in context).

Solution: Draw each word as a single string using arabic_reshaper.reshape(word) + get_display(reshaped, base_dir='R'), then render the entire shaped word at once with PIL anchor 'la'. AOI widths are derived from draw.textlength() on
the shaped string, not from individual character renders.

---
2. RTL rendering architecture

Problem: PIL has no native RTL support.

Solution: A custom RTL rendering loop: current_x starts at the right margin and decrements leftward for each word. Each word is placed at (current_x - word_width, y). Space AOIs are placed to the RIGHT of each word (not left), so no
trailing space appears at the left edge of lines.

---
3. En dash rendering as tofu box (□)

Problem: FreeFarsi-Mono lacks U+2013 (en dash).

Solution: In create_other_screens(), replace \u2013 with - when language is fa or ar, guarded by isinstance(text, str) to handle pandas NaN from empty Excel cells.

---
4. Punctuation on wrong side of Eastern Arabic digits

Problem: Commas and periods appeared to the RIGHT of digits like ۱۲ instead of the left — because Eastern Arabic digits have BiDi class EN (European Number), not a strong RTL class, so get_display() defaulted to LTR base direction.

Solution: Pass base_dir='R' explicitly to every get_display() call in the Arabic rendering path.

---
5. Parenthesis mirroring for single-word cases

Problem: A closing ) at the end of an LTR word (e.g. MultiplEYE)) ended up visually on the wrong side in RTL context.

Solution: For LTR words with trailing closing brackets, strip the bracket, mirror it via _BIDI_MIRROR, and prepend it to the render string — so ) becomes ( at the left edge of the word. Also handles a trailing . before the bracket
(e.g. word).).

---
6. Multi-word English phrase word order

Problem: Multi-word English phrases embedded in Farsi (e.g. "European Cooperation in Science and Technology") appeared in reversed word order because each word was placed individually right-to-left.

Solution: Before the word rendering loop, detect runs of consecutive Latin-alpha words in words_in_line and reverse them in-place. When placed right-to-left, the reversed run renders in correct left-to-right reading order.

---
7. Parentheses in multi-word English phrases

Problem: After reversing the LTR run (fix 6), a closing bracket on the last English word (e.g. Technology)) ended up in the middle of the phrase instead of at the left edge.

Solution: During the LTR-run pre-processing (before reversal), detect a trailing closing bracket on the last word of the run, strip it, mirror it, and prepend the mirrored bracket to the first word of the run. After reversal, the
mirrored bracket lands on the leftmost word — the correct visual position for a closing bracket in RTL context.

---
8. Equal-size AOI boxes with ligature handling

Problem: The لا (lam-alef) ligature collapses 2 original characters into 1 glyph of 1 standard character width, so dividing total word width by character count gave misaligned boxes (≈12px instead of the 15px monospaced standard).

Solution: Each original character always gets a box of std_char_w (= draw.textlength('م', font=font) = 15px). When actual_word_width ≠ n_chars × std_char_w (due to a ligature), a separate _LIGA_SPACE_ (or _EXTRA_SPACE_) AOI is added
for the difference, drawn in blue in the overlay image and labeled distinctly in the AOI CSV.
